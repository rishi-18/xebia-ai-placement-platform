## 1. Executive Summary

This Stakeholder Analysis is a foundational deliverable for the **AI-Powered Placement Management Platform** — a next-generation system designed to automate and optimize the end-to-end placement (internship/job) lifecycle for academic institutions, students, and recruiters.

The purpose of this analysis is to:
- Identify all parties with a vested interest in the platform
- Understand their goals and pain points
- Assess their level of influence and interest
- Define engagement strategies to ensure smooth adoption and sustained value delivery

---

## 2. Stakeholder Identification

Stakeholders are classified into **three tiers** based on directness of interaction and degree of impact on platform success.

### 2.1 Primary Stakeholders (Direct Users)

| Stakeholder | Role | Platform Interaction |
|-------------|------|----------------------|
| **Students / Job Seekers** | Final-year and post-grad students seeking internships or full-time employment | Resume upload, AI job matching, interview scheduling, offer tracking |
| **Placement Coordinators** | College staff managing company relations, student readiness, and drive logistics | Dashboard management, drive creation, student shortlisting, analytics |
| **Recruiters / HR Teams** | Industry professionals sourcing candidates from the institution | JD posting, AI-filtered candidate lists, interview slot booking, offer letters |
| **Department HODs** | Heads of Department overseeing placement performance by branch | Branch-level analytics, report generation, approval workflows |

### 2.2 Secondary Stakeholders (Indirect Impact)

| Stakeholder | Role | Interest in Platform |
|-------------|------|----------------------|
| **College Administration / Principal** | Institutional leadership overseeing academic and placement policy | Overall placement rate KPIs, institutional branding and rankings |
| **Alumni Network** | Graduated students who can act as referrers or mentors | Mentorship modules, referral tracking, alumni-drive participation |
| **Training & Development Team** | Staff providing aptitude, soft-skills, and domain training to students | Skill gap analysis reports, AI-suggested training paths |
| **Parents / Guardians** | Family members with emotional and financial stake in student placement | Placement status notifications (optional parent portal / email alerts) |

### 2.3 Tertiary Stakeholders (Peripheral Influence)

| Stakeholder | Role | Connection to Platform |
|-------------|------|------------------------|
| **University / Affiliating Body** | Governing body setting academic and compliance standards | Compliance reporting, accreditation data, policy enforcement |
| **Government / Regulatory Bodies** | Entities like AICTE, UGC, or labour departments | Data privacy compliance (IT Act), employment statistics reporting |
| **Technology Vendors / Integrators** | Cloud providers, AI API vendors, payment gateways | Platform infrastructure, uptime SLAs, AI model performance |

## 4. Detailed Stakeholder Profiles

### 4.1 Students / Job Seekers
**Role:** Primary User | **Power:** Low | **Interest:** High | **Strategy:** Keep Informed

#### Goals & Needs
- Get matched to relevant job/internship opportunities quickly
- Receive AI-generated resume and profile improvement suggestions
- Access real-time status updates on applications and interviews
- Practice with AI-mock interviews and aptitude test modules
- Receive personalised career roadmap and upskilling suggestions

#### Pain Points & Challenges
- Lack of visibility into which companies are visiting and when
- Generic resumes not tailored to specific JD requirements
- No centralised portal — managing everything through WhatsApp/email
- No structured interview prep resources available from institution
- Uncertainty about placement status and next steps in the process

#### Success Criteria
- At least 80% of registered students receive at least one interview call
- AI match accuracy rated 4/5 or above by students within 3 months
- Application status visible in real-time with zero manual follow-ups needed

#### Engagement Strategy
- Onboarding tutorial and guided walkthrough on first login
- Weekly AI-generated placement tips and company arrival alerts via push notifications
- Feedback collection after each drive for platform improvement

---

### 4.2 Placement Coordinators
**Role:** Platform Administrator | **Power:** High | **Interest:** High | **Strategy:** Manage Closely

#### Goals & Needs
- Automate repetitive tasks: shortlisting, scheduling, communication
- Maintain a single source of truth for all placement drives
- Generate accurate placement reports for HODs and administration
- Track and improve student placement readiness over time
- Manage recruiter relationships and campus visit logistics

#### Pain Points & Challenges
- Managing hundreds of students manually via spreadsheets and emails
- Difficulty tracking which students applied where and their outcomes
- No automated communication — every notification is manual
- Data scattered across departments; consolidated reports take days
- Last-minute recruiter changes cause scheduling chaos

#### Success Criteria
- Placement coordinator administrative workload reduced by 60%
- All active drives visible in a single dashboard with zero data gaps
- Automated reports generated in under 2 minutes vs. 2 days manually

#### Engagement Strategy
- Dedicated onboarding session and admin training workshop
- Monthly coordinator review calls to gather usage feedback
- Priority support channel (dedicated email/Slack) for coordinators

---

### 4.3 Recruiters / HR Teams
**Role:** External Hiring Partner | **Power:** High | **Interest:** High | **Strategy:** Manage Closely

#### Goals & Needs
- Access a pre-filtered pool of qualified, relevant candidates fast
- Post job descriptions and receive AI-ranked applicant shortlists
- Schedule interviews seamlessly without back-and-forth emails
- Access student profiles with verified skills, projects, and transcripts
- Get analytics on hire quality and campus ROI over time

#### Pain Points & Challenges
- Screening hundreds of unfiltered CVs to find relevant candidates
- Poor communication and coordination with placement cells
- No standardised profile format — comparing candidates is tedious
- Lengthy scheduling process via emails instead of an integrated calendar
- Difficulty measuring return on campus hiring investment

#### Success Criteria
- Time-to-shortlist reduced by 70% compared to manual screening
- 90%+ recruiter satisfaction score in post-drive surveys
- Full interview cycle completed within the platform without external tools

#### Engagement Strategy
- Dedicated recruiter portal with lightweight onboarding guide
- Account manager assigned for top-tier recruiting partners
- Quarterly recruiter satisfaction and NPS surveys

---

### 4.4 Department HODs
**Role:** Departmental Oversight | **Power:** High | **Interest:** Medium | **Strategy:** Manage Closely

#### Goals & Needs
- Track branch-wise and department-wise placement outcomes
- Identify underperforming students early and trigger interventions
- Access real-time dashboards without relying on manual reports
- Ensure fair and transparent shortlisting across all students
- Contribute to improving institutional accreditation metrics

#### Pain Points & Challenges
- No visibility into real-time placement progress per department
- Placement data delivered manually and often delayed
- Cannot identify skill gaps at a department level proactively
- No audit trail for placement decisions or student-company matching

#### Success Criteria
- Department-level dashboards accessible in real-time
- Proactive skill gap alerts generated at least 4 weeks before placement season
- Branch placement rate improvement of 15% year-on-year

#### Engagement Strategy
- Quarterly briefings on branch-level placement performance
- Custom report templates built for NAAC/NBA accreditation
- Escalation pathway to product team for critical concerns

---

### 4.5 College Administration / Principal
**Role:** Strategic Oversight | **Power:** High | **Interest:** Medium | **Strategy:** Keep Satisfied

#### Goals & Needs
- Improve and showcase institutional placement rates year over year
- Comply with accreditation bodies requiring placement performance data
- Attract premium recruiters to boost institutional reputation
- Ensure ethical, fair, and transparent placement processes

#### Pain Points & Challenges
- Placement statistics compiled manually and often inaccurate
- Compliance reports for NAAC/NBA require extensive data collection
- No audit trail for placement decisions and student-offer matching
- Difficulty benchmarking performance against peer institutions

#### Success Criteria
- Real-time executive dashboard showing live placement KPIs
- Compliance-ready reports exportable in one click
- Year-on-year placement rate improvement of 15% in the first academic year

#### Engagement Strategy
- Quarterly executive briefings on platform performance
- Custom reporting for NAAC/NBA accreditation needs
- Dedicated institutional account manager

---

### 4.6 Alumni Network
**Role:** Mentor & Referrer | **Power:** Low | **Interest:** High | **Strategy:** Keep Informed

#### Goals & Needs
- Give back to the institution through mentorship and referrals
- Hire qualified juniors for their own organisations
- Stay connected with the placement cell and institution

#### Pain Points & Challenges
- No structured platform to offer mentorship or referrals
- Disconnected from current students and placement activities
- No incentive or recognition mechanism for alumni participation

#### Success Criteria
- Active alumni mentor pool of 100+ within the first year
- 10% of student placements attributable to alumni referrals

#### Engagement Strategy
- Dedicated alumni portal module with mentor profiles
- Semester-wise alumni engagement campaigns via email
- Recognition badges and certificates for active mentors

---

### 4.7 Training & Development Team
**Role:** Skill Enablement | **Power:** Low | **Interest:** High | **Strategy:** Keep Informed

#### Goals & Needs
- Understand skill gaps across student cohorts to tailor training
- Align training calendar with upcoming placement drives
- Measure training effectiveness through placement outcomes

#### Pain Points & Challenges
- No data-driven insight into which skills students are lacking
- Training calendar not aligned with placement season schedule
- No feedback loop between training outcomes and placement results

#### Success Criteria
- AI-generated skill gap reports available before every training cycle
- Training completion rates trackable within the platform

#### Engagement Strategy
- Access to aggregated skill gap analytics dashboard
- Bi-semester sync with placement coordinators on training priorities

## 5. Stakeholder Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Low student adoption of the platform | High | Students bypass platform; AI models underperform due to sparse data | Gamification, rewards, mandatory registration via institution policy |
| Recruiter resistance to new portal workflow | Medium | Low recruiter engagement; drives conducted outside the system | Frictionless UX, dedicated account manager, ROI data shared quarterly |
| Coordinator data entry errors & inconsistency | Medium | Inaccurate placement reports; student-company mismatches | Automated data validation, import from existing Excel files, audit logs |
| Regulatory non-compliance (Data Privacy) | Low | Legal liability; loss of institutional trust | DPDP Act compliance audit, role-based access control, data encryption |
| Administration under-prioritising the project | Medium | Funding cuts, low buy-in, delayed rollout | Regular executive dashboards, ROI reports, accreditation tie-in |
